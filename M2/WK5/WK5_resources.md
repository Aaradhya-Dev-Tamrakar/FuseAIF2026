Follow this course structure to complete week 5 of this series.

---

**Phase 1: Base Learners & Core Theory (The Naive Approach)**

- The Anatomy of a Decision Tree: Splitting criteria (Gini, Entropy), decision boundaries, and hyper-sensitivity to noise.
- Link Bias and Variance with Trees (experiencing overfitting on raw data).

**Phase 2: Data Prep & Evaluation (Fixing the Naive Model)**

- Feature Engineering: Handling imbalanced and missing data.
- AUROC, Precision, Recall, F1 and their significance. Why not just accuracy? (Seeing why the naive model's high accuracy was an illusion.)

**Phase 3: Building the Crowd (Ensembles)**

- Ensemble Foundations: Why a "wisdom of the crowd" approach works (diversity + accuracy).
- Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating): Parallel ensembling to reduce variance.
- Understand random forest and differentiate it from bagging, listing the steps involved in creating a random forest.

**Phase 4: State-of-the-Art & Optimization**

- Modern Frameworks: The math and mechanics behind XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost with regularization.
- Cross-validation.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Grid Search, Bayesian search.

**Phase 5: Workflow Management & Interpretability**

- Sklearn Pipeline (Transitioning from manual steps to an encapsulated, leak-proof workflow).
- SHAP and feature importance.

**Phase 6: Deployment Prep**

- Save and load the model with versioning and serialization type.
